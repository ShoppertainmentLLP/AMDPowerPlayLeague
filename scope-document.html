Section 01

Document Purpose

This Statement of Work (SoW) defines the delivery scope for the Sales Excellence League (SEL) platform for Microsoft. It describes a three-phase delivery approach, with the first phase delivered as a closed pilot to validate the core experience before broader rollout.

The document is intended as a shared reference between Microsoft and Shoppertainment LLP. It sets out what is included in each phase, what is explicitly excluded, the assumptions and dependencies that delivery relies on, and the acceptance criteria for each phase. It is a living document — scope for Phases 2 and 3 will be confirmed and detailed further following the outcomes of the Phase 1 pilot.

At a glance: Phase 1 (Pilot) integrates Device Partner University content with an AI course navigator and a gamified leaderboard for a closed user group. Phase 2 adds Device Partner Center marketing assets and a knowledge base. Phase 3 activates the live reward shop.
Section 02

Solution Context

SEL is a facilitator and engagement layer that sits on top of Microsoft's existing partner content platforms. It does not host or replace Microsoft content. Instead, it curates that content, applies a gamification layer to drive engagement, and uses AI to help resellers find what they need quickly.

The two Microsoft content platforms SEL connects to are:

  • Device Partner University (DPU) — Microsoft's training and certification platform, hosting MS Learn courses, Master Training Team sessions, and webinars. Addressed in Phase 1.
  • Device Partner Center (DPC) — Microsoft's hub for sales and marketing collateral, campaign assets, and partner enablement material. Addressed in Phase 2.

SEL's value to Microsoft is measurable: every reseller engaging through SEL is a reseller engaging with DPU and DPC content. The platform turns Microsoft's existing content investment into trackable partner engagement, certification uptake, and sales enablement activity.

Section 03

Phased Delivery Overview

Delivery is structured into three sequential phases. Each phase produces a usable, demonstrable increment of the platform. Phase 1 is delivered as a pilot to a closed user group; Phases 2 and 3 progressively expand functionality and audience.

Section 04

Phase 1 — DPU Integration & Pilot Launch

P1
Pilot — Closed User Group
DPU integration · AI course navigator · Leaderboard · Reward shop preview

4.1 Objective

Validate the core SEL experience with a limited pilot audience — reached via a private sign-up link Microsoft distributes to its intended participants — before broader rollout. Phase 1 proves the platform's ability to drive engagement with DPU training content, demonstrates the AI course navigator, and establishes the gamification and leaderboard mechanics.

4.2 In Scope

A. DPU Content Integration

  • MS Learn courses — relevant courses surfaced within SEL as curated, role-based learning paths, deep-linked to the source content on DPU / MS Learn.
  • Master Training Team meetings — scheduled training sessions surfaced in the platform with details and registration links.
  • Webinars (conducted and hosted by Microsoft) — Microsoft-hosted webinar sessions surfaced in the platform with registration or replay links. SEL displays and deep-links to these sessions; webinar conduct, hosting, and streaming remain with Microsoft.
  • A curated, minimalist content structure so users are guided to relevant content rather than browsing an overwhelming catalogue.

B. Authentication & Access (Pilot Approach)

  • Registration and login handled by a standalone, platform-managed authentication module for the pilot — an interim approach independent of MS Learn.
  • Self-registration via a private pilot sign-up link — partners register themselves using the link Microsoft shares with its pilot audience. No admin-managed email allowlist is required.
  • Audience control sits with Microsoft via link distribution — the link is shared only with intended pilot participants; the platform itself does not gate by email.
  • Role-based access (e.g. participant, administrator) to support pilot administration.
Future state: The production target is single sign-on (SSO) parity with MS Learn. SSO integration is not in scope for the Phase 1 pilot — a platform-managed authentication module is the agreed interim solution. MS Learn SSO will be scoped and scheduled as a post-pilot activity.

C. AI Course Navigator

  • An AI assistant that helps users find the right course based on their learning path or training requirements.
  • The assistant is conversational — it can ask clarifying questions to better understand a user's requirement before recommending content.
  • The AI knowledge base is built from the MS Learn course catalogue, upcoming webinars, and past webinars provided by Microsoft.
  • Recommendations deep-link the user directly to the relevant course or session on DPU.

D. AI Certificate Validation & Points Award

  • After completing a course, users upload their certificate of completion to the platform as proof of training.
  • An AI validation step reviews each uploaded certificate — checking that it is a genuine completion certificate and matches the claimed course — before points are awarded.
  • On successful validation, points / XP are awarded automatically for the completed course, feeding the user's balance and leaderboard position.
  • Certificates that cannot be validated automatically are flagged for administrator review, keeping a human check in the loop.

E. Leaderboard

  • Global leaderboard ranking participants by points / XP earned through training engagement.
  • Regional filters allowing the leaderboard to be viewed by region.
  • Points / XP earned from validated course completions and platform engagement activities, displayed against each participant.

F. Reward Shop — Preview Only

  • The reward shop (Partner Growth Catalogue) is visible within the platform so pilot users can see the rewards available to them.
  • It is presented in a "Coming Soon" state — browsing is enabled, but redemption is not transactional in Phase 1.
  • Live redemption is delivered in Phase 3 (see Section 6).

G. Marketing Landing Page

  • Public-facing landing page introducing the SEL platform to Microsoft partners and driving sign-up into the pilot.
  • Content creation — copy, messaging, value proposition, and the four-pillar narrative (Train, Sell, Convince, Earn) crafted for the partner audience.
  • Section design — hero, live activity feed, pillars, content preview, leaderboard preview, AI coach showcase, reward catalogue preview, and FAQ.
  • Sign-in flow wiring the landing page CTAs to the platform's authentication module.
  • Single-page, responsive, brand-aligned with Microsoft's partner identity.

4.3 Phase 1 Scope Boundaries

Included in Phase 1
  • DPU content integration (courses, training meetings, Microsoft-hosted webinars surfaced as links)
  • Platform-managed registration & login
  • Self-registration via private pilot sign-up link
  • Conversational AI course navigator
  • AI certificate validation & automatic points award
  • Global + regional leaderboard
  • Points / XP accrual
  • Reward shop in preview ("Coming Soon") state
  • Marketing landing page (content + design)
Not in Phase 1
  • MS Learn SSO (interim platform-managed auth used instead)
  • DPC marketing assets & knowledge base (Phase 2)
  • Live reward redemption & fulfilment (Phase 3)
  • Public marketing of the pilot sign-up link
  • Production-scale rollout beyond the pilot cohort

4.4 Phase 1 Deliverables

  • SEL pilot platform deployed and accessible to the closed pilot group.
  • DPU content integrated and presented as curated learning paths.
  • Microsoft-hosted webinars surfaced in the platform via registration / replay deep-links.
  • AI course navigator trained on the supplied MS Learn catalogue and webinar data.
  • AI certificate validation flow, with automatic points award on successful validation and administrator review for flagged certificates.
  • Global and regional leaderboard, live with pilot participant data.
  • Reward shop visible in preview state.
  • Pilot administration view for managing users, reviewing flagged certificates, and monitoring engagement.
  • Marketing landing page (content + design) introducing the platform and driving sign-up.

4.5 Phase 1 Exit Criteria

Phase 1 is considered complete when the pilot platform is live for the closed pilot group, all five core capability areas (DPU content, AI course navigator, AI certificate validation, leaderboard, reward preview) are demonstrably functional, and a pilot feedback review has been conducted with Microsoft to inform Phase 2 planning.

Section 05

Phase 2 — DPC Integration

P2
Sales & Marketing Enablement
Marketing asset discovery · Knowledge base · AI extended to DPC

5.1 Objective

Extend the SEL platform to cover Microsoft's sales and marketing enablement content from Device Partner Center, so resellers can find and use the right collateral as easily as they find training in Phase 1.

5.2 In Scope

  • DPC integration — connecting the platform to Device Partner Center so users can identify and download marketing assets relevant to their needs.
  • Marketing asset discovery — curated, searchable access to sales collateral and campaign assets, rather than an unstructured file library.
  • Knowledge base — a structured library of FAQs, objection-handling guidance, and related sales-readiness content.
  • AI extension — the AI assistant from Phase 1 extended to understand and recommend DPC marketing assets and knowledge base content, not just training.

5.3 Phase 2 Deliverables

  • DPC marketing asset discovery and download integrated into SEL.
  • Knowledge base (FAQs, objection handling) live and searchable.
  • AI assistant extended to cover DPC asset and knowledge base recommendations.
Scope confirmation: The detailed scope for Phase 2 will be confirmed following the Phase 1 pilot review, incorporating pilot feedback and any changes to DPC content structure or access.
Section 06

Phase 3 — Rewards Go-Live

P3
Live Reward Redemption
Reward shop activated · Redemption workflow · Fulfilment

6.1 Objective

Activate the reward shop so participants can redeem the points / XP they have accumulated since Phase 1, completing the full earn-and-redeem programme loop.

6.2 In Scope

  • Live reward shop — the Partner Growth Catalogue moves from preview state to fully transactional.
  • Redemption workflow — users can redeem accumulated points / XP for catalogue rewards.
  • Administration & approval — an admin workflow to review, approve, and track redemptions.
  • Fulfilment handling — the process for delivering redeemed rewards to participants.

6.3 Phase 3 Deliverables

  • Reward shop activated for live redemption.
  • End-to-end redemption and approval workflow operational.
  • Reward fulfilment process in place.
Scope confirmation: Reward catalogue contents, point values, fulfilment logistics, and any procurement or tax considerations will be confirmed with Microsoft ahead of Phase 3 delivery.

Section 08

Assumptions & Dependencies

Delivery of the scope above relies on the following assumptions and dependencies:

  • Microsoft provides the complete MS Learn course catalogue and course details — including course titles, descriptions, learning paths, durations, and links — in a usable, structured format (export or feed). This catalogue is the source data for both the curated content structure and the AI course navigator.
  • Microsoft conducts and hosts all webinars at its end and provides the corresponding webinar data and registration / replay links — upcoming and past — together with details of Master Training Team meetings to be surfaced in the platform.
  • Microsoft distributes the private pilot sign-up link to the intended pilot audience and controls who receives it. Self-registration is enabled on the platform; no email allowlist is maintained.
  • The format and structure of course completion certificates to be validated are confirmed, with representative sample certificates provided.
  • The technical means of integrating with DPU (and, in Phase 2, DPC) — APIs, content feeds, or deep-link structures — are made available by Microsoft.
  • Branding guidelines are provided.
  • MS Learn SSO is treated as a post-pilot activity; a platform-managed authentication module is accepted for the pilot.
  • Reward catalogue, point values, and fulfilment approach for Phase 3 are confirmed with Microsoft ahead of that phase.
  • Phase 2 and Phase 3 scope is confirmed following the respective preceding phase review.
Content responsibility: The MS Learn course catalogue and course details are to be supplied by Microsoft. Manually researching, hunting, or compiling the course list is not part of this quote — the engagement curates, structures, and surfaces the catalogue Microsoft provides; it does not source it.
Section 09

Out of Scope

The following are explicitly excluded from this engagement unless added by written change:

  • Sourcing, manually researching, hunting, or compiling the MS Learn course catalogue / course list — this is provided by Microsoft. The engagement curates and structures the supplied catalogue; it does not build it.
  • Hosting, conducting, or streaming webinar sessions — webinars are conducted and hosted by Microsoft at its end. SEL surfaces them as registration / replay deep-links only.
  • MS Learn single sign-on (SSO) integration — scoped separately as a post-pilot activity.
  • Hosting or replication of Microsoft training videos or content within SEL — content remains on DPU / DPC.
  • Creation of new training, webinar, or marketing content (beyond the landing page content covered in Phase 1, Section 4.2.G).
  • Public marketing of the pilot sign-up link — link distribution remains private and is managed by Microsoft.
  • Procurement of physical reward inventory (handled via the Phase 3 fulfilment approach, confirmed separately).

Section 01

The Platform

The AMD Power Play League platform is the solution delivered under this engagement. It is a partner engagement platform that brings AMD's training and sales content together in one place, wraps it in a gamified experience, and uses AI to help every partner rep find what they need — so training gets completed, certifications get earned, and reps are ready to pitch AMD with confidence.

It is delivered as a single phase covering the full platform: training integration, an AI assistant, automatic course-completion tracking, the gamification engine and live leaderboard, sales & marketing asset access, a live reward shop, the public marketing landing page, and a role-based command center.

What this document covers: the platform solution and the agreed single-phase delivery scope — capabilities, deliverables, technical approach, assumptions, and timeline.
Section 02

Solution Approach

The platform is a facilitator and engagement layer on top of the AMD Partner Portal — AMD's single hub for all partner content. It does not host or replace AMD content — it curates it, applies a gamification layer to drive engagement, and uses AI to help partners find what they need quickly. The portal is the source for both content types the platform works with:

  • Training & certification — courses, curriculums, and certifications.
  • Sales & marketing — sales and marketing collateral, campaign assets, and partner enablement material.

The platform's value is measurable: every reseller engaging through it is a reseller engaging with AMD Partner Portal content. It turns AMD's existing content investment into trackable engagement, certification uptake, and sales-enablement activity.

Section 03

Platform Capabilities

Full Platform — One Phase
Training integration · AI · Gamification & leaderboard · Marketing assets · Live rewards · Landing page · Admin
A. Training & Certification Content
AMD Partner Portal courses surfaced as curated, role-based learning paths (Client PC & Data Center tracks), deep-linked to the source content on the portal and organised by curriculum and certification (foundational, vertical, master). A minimalist structure that guides users instead of overwhelming them.
B. Authentication & Access
Platform-managed registration & login with self-registration via the private sign-up link AMD distributes. Role-based access (participant, team lead, administrator). AMD Partner Portal SSO available as an optional integration where API access is provided.
C. AI Assistant
A conversational AI assistant that helps users find the right course for their requirement, asking clarifying questions where needed. Built from the AMD Partner Portal course catalogue and certification structure, and deep-links straight to the relevant course.
D. Course Completion & Points Award
Completed training is verified and points are awarded automatically. Where the AMD Partner Portal exposes an API or data feed for course completions and certifications, the platform consumes it directly as the source of truth. Where it does not, the platform falls back to AI certificate validation — users upload their completion certificate and an AI step verifies it before points are awarded, with anything it cannot confirm flagged for administrator review. Both paths deliver the same outcome.
E. Gamification Engine
A configurable points and milestone engine with individual scoring and team bonuses, plus team / squad structures and engagement nudges. Scoring rules, tiers, and milestones are administered to match AMD's program design.
F. Live Leaderboard
Global, regional, and team leaderboards ranking participants by points earned, in real time from validated completions, certifications, pitch challenges, and engagement.
G. Sales & Marketing Enablement
From the same AMD Partner Portal — curated, searchable marketing-asset discovery & download, plus a knowledge base (FAQs, objection handling, battlecards). The AI assistant is extended to recommend these assets, not just training.
H. Live Reward Shop
A live, transactional reward catalogue — participants redeem accumulated points for rewards, with an administrator review, approval, and tracking workflow, and a fulfilment process for delivery.
I. Marketing Landing Page
A public-facing landing page introducing the platform and driving sign-up — content + design (hero, live activity feed, pillars, content & leaderboard previews, AI coach showcase, reward preview, FAQ), wired into the platform sign-in.
J. Role-Based Command Center
Role-based access — each user sees only the data and controls relevant to their role (e.g. participant, team lead, regional manager, administrator). Covers user & team management, scoring and milestone configuration, completion / certificate review, reward approvals, content curation, and engagement reporting — each surfaced to the right role.
On completion tracking (Capability D): the platform always delivers the same outcome — automatic, validated points award. Whether this is achieved via a direct AMD Partner Portal API/feed or via the AI certificate-validation fallback is determined purely by the access AMD makes available, and does not change the scope, deliverables, or price of this engagement. The AI fallback exists so the outcome holds even when no API is available; it is not a separable add-on.

Section 08

Assumptions & Dependencies

Delivery of the scope above relies on the following assumptions and dependencies:

  • AMD provides the complete training catalogue and curriculums — the Client PC and Data Center modules, the certification structure (foundational, vertical, master), and the Pitch Perfect definitions — with titles, descriptions, learning paths, and links, in a usable, structured format. This is the source data for the curated content structure and the AI assistant.
  • AMD provides the program rules to configure — tier prerequisites (people per organisation and modules per person), point values, and team milestones — per AMD's program design.
  • Completion tracking follows the access AMD provides. Where the AMD Partner Portal exposes an API or data feed for course completions and certifications, the platform consumes it directly. Where it does not, AMD confirms the format of course completion certificates (with representative samples) so the AI validation fallback can verify them. Either way the outcome and scope are unchanged.
  • The technical means of integrating with the AMD Partner Portal — APIs, content feeds, or deep-link structures, for both training and sales / marketing content — are made available by AMD.
  • AMD distributes the private sign-up link to its partner base and controls who receives it. Self-registration is enabled; no email allowlist is maintained.
  • Reward catalogue, point values, and fulfilment approach are confirmed with AMD, including any procurement or tax considerations for physical rewards.
  • Branding guidelines are provided.
Content responsibility: The AMD Partner Portal course catalogue and Partner Portal assets are supplied by AMD. Manually researching, hunting, or compiling the course list or asset library is not part of this quote — the engagement curates, structures, and surfaces the content AMD provides; it does not source it.
Section 09

Out of Scope

The following are explicitly excluded unless added by written change:

  • Sourcing, researching, or compiling the AMD Partner Portal catalogue / asset library — provided by AMD; the engagement curates and structures it.
  • Hosting or replication of AMD training videos or content within the platform — content remains on the AMD Partner Portal.
  • Creation of new training or marketing content (beyond the landing page content in Section 3.I).
  • Public marketing of the sign-up link — distribution remains managed by AMD.
  • Procurement of physical reward inventory — handled via the fulfilment approach, confirmed separately.
